I am an incoming PhD student in Public Policy (Economics Track) at Harvard Kennedy School, where I will work with Professor Desmond Ang. My research lies at the intersection of urban economics, public finance, and economic history. I study how public institutions and policies shape housing markets, immigration outcomes, and educational attainment, with particular attention to distributional consequences across race and income.

My current working paper examines the effects of Massachusetts' Community Preservation Act — a locally levied property tax surcharge that funds affordable housing, land preservation, recreation, and historic conservation — on housing supply and neighborhood composition. In ongoing work with Elizabeth Cascio and Ethan Lewis at Dartmouth, I study how the postwar school construction boom in the American South shaped Black students' educational trajectories. Separately, with Harufumi Nakazawa and Ariana Rodriguez, I investigate how the opening of ICE detention centers shapes local political attitudes toward immigration.

Before Harvard, I was a pre-doctoral fellow in the Department of Economics at Dartmouth College, advising with Professors Elizabeth Cascio and Ethan Lewis. I graduated magna cum laude with distinction from Amherst College in 2024, where I majored in economics. Earlier, I worked as a research assistant at the University of Massachusetts Amherst and as a summer intern at the Federal Reserve Bank of Boston.
